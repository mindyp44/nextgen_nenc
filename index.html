About NextGen NENC

Not one church's thing. A regional connection point.

NextGen NENC is designed to help students and parents find real connection across Northeast North Carolina. The heart is simple: make it easier for students to find a youth group, make friends, ask questions, and take a next step in faith.

Why it exists

Families often do not know where to start. Some students are new to church, some are looking for a better fit, and some simply need a safe place where they can belong.

Who it serves

Students, parents, youth leaders, and churches across the region. The goal is not to compete with local churches. The goal is to help students connect to them.

Built Across Denominations

Your tradition matters here.

IGNITE NENC is being shaped by youth leaders from across the denominational spectrum. The teaching content, event format, and values of this gathering reflect input from the churches it serves. Not a single tradition speaking over others.

We will not use this stage to teach into denominational distinctives. What you will hear is what every church we work with agrees on: that students matter, that Jesus is worth following, and that no student has to have it all figured out to belong.

Common Questions

FAQs for students, parents, and church leaders.

Everything students, parents, and church leaders need to know.

NG

NextGen NENC Platform

Questions about the directory and connection platform.

Why does this platform exist?

Students across Northeast North Carolina often do not know where to start when looking for a youth group. This platform makes it simple to discover real youth ministry options in one place.

Is NextGen NENC owned by one church?

No. It is a shared connection tool designed to support local churches across the region, not compete with them.

Is the platform free?

Yes. The goal is to make it easy for students, families, and churches to connect without cost being a barrier.

ST

Students

Questions students may be wondering before they show up.

What if I do not know anyone?

That is exactly why this exists. Youth leaders expect new students and want to help them feel welcome.

Can I bring a friend?

Yes. Most youth groups welcome friends, even if they do not attend that church.

Do I have to commit?

No. Visiting once does not obligate you to join anything. That said, our hope is that every student finds a youth group they want to keep coming back to. Real community is built over time, and that is exactly what this is all about.

What if I am not sure what I believe?

You can still come. A good youth group is a place to ask questions, learn, and be treated with respect.

CH

Already Rooted in a Church

For families who are connected to a church but looking for more for their student.

Can my child attend a youth group at a church we don't attend?

Yes, and this is more common than most people realize. Many youth groups actively welcome students from other churches or no church at all. A student attending youth group elsewhere is not a betrayal of your home church. It is simply finding the right community for that season of life.

Should I talk to our home church pastor about this?

We encourage it. Most pastors would rather a student be connected somewhere than disconnected from community entirely. A good pastor will support a student finding the right place for this season of life, and many will even help point you toward a youth group that fits your family well.

What if our church has different beliefs than the youth group?

That is worth a conversation with the youth leader before your student attends. A good youth leader will be honest about what they teach and respect the convictions your family brings. You can also use the FAQ section on this site to see how IGNITE NENC handles denominational differences at the event level.

My church is small and doesn't have enough students for a youth group. What should we do?

This is one of the most common situations in rural areas like NENC. Some families choose to send their student to a nearby youth group during the week while continuing to worship at their home church on Sundays. Others have used this directory to connect with neighboring churches and explored informal joint youth nights. There is no single right answer, but connection matters, and this platform exists to help you find it.

PR

Parents

What you need to know before your child gets involved.

Does my child need to attend a church to participate?

No. Students can come with a group, with friends, or on their own. All are welcome regardless of church background.

How can I help my child find a youth group?

Browse the church cards together, check church websites and social media, and contact the youth leader before your child attends.

How is safety handled?

Every listed church should have adult leadership on-site. Contact should go through appropriate church channels, never private adult-to-student messaging.

What actually happens at youth group?

Most groups include hangout time, games, worship, a relevant message, and small group discussion.

What does a first visit actually look like?

Here is what most first visits look like, step by step: You pull up and walk in. Most youth groups have someone at the door or near the entrance. Your student will be greeted, probably asked their name and grade, and pointed toward where students are hanging out. There is usually free time or games before anything formal starts, which gives your student a chance to ease in without pressure. The main program follows: worship, a message, maybe small groups. Then it wraps up and students head out. The whole thing is usually 60 to 90 minutes. Most students say the hardest part is walking in. After that it gets easier fast.

Should I let the youth leader know my child is coming for the first time?

Yes, and this is one of the most helpful things you can do. A quick message to the youth leader before your student visits means they already know your child's name when they walk in. A good youth leader will often identify another student ahead of time to help connect your child with the group. That one small step can turn a nervous first visit into a genuinely warm welcome. You can reach the youth leader through the church links on each card in the directory.

CL

Church Leaders

How churches can participate and keep information current.

How can our church be listed?

A youth leader or approved church representative can submit youth ministry details, meeting times, leader contact information, and social links for review.

Do we control our profile information?

Yes. Each church should be able to keep its own listing current so meeting times, links, contacts, and special event details stay accurate.

Does our church have to be a certain size?

No. Small groups, larger student ministries, rural churches, and regional ministries can all be helpful options for students looking for a place to belong.

IG

IGNITE NENC Event

Questions specifically about the regional IGNITE gathering.

What is IGNITE NENC?

IGNITE NENC is a one-night regional student gathering for worship, biblical teaching, connection, and clear next steps with local youth groups.

Is IGNITE replacing youth group?

No. IGNITE is designed to strengthen local youth groups by pointing students back to ongoing community in their area.

Who can attend?

Middle and high school students from across Northeast North Carolina are welcome, whether they already attend church or are brand new.

Can a church bring a whole youth group?

Yes. The event is intended for students to come with youth groups, friends, parents, or trusted leaders.

How do you choose speakers for IGNITE?

Speakers are selected based on their ability to connect students to the person of Jesus Christ and the authority of Scripture. We look for communicators who can speak clearly and compellingly across denominational lines, not representatives of any single tradition.

What topics will be taught?

Teaching at IGNITE focuses on what every church we work with agrees on: the person of Jesus, the relevance of Scripture, and what it looks like to follow God as a student in real life. We intentionally avoid topics that are genuine points of denominational disagreement.

Who is involved in shaping the teaching content?

The IGNITE planning team includes youth leaders from different denominational backgrounds across NENC. Teaching topics, speaker selection, and event format are developed collaboratively so that no single tradition drives the content.

What if my church disagrees with something that is said at the event?

We want to know. The goal of IGNITE is to serve local churches, not create friction with them. If something communicated at the event conflicts with your church's convictions, reach out to us directly. That kind of feedback makes the event better.

Still have questions? Use the Contact button on any church card to reach out directly to that youth leader.
